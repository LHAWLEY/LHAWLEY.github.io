Recursion

January 11, 2016

Recursion is the idea that a function or method can call itself, passing in a different argument each time until it reaches a certain threshold. Recursion is often used to replace loops in a method.

Recursion breaks down a problem into smaller pieces. In computer science this algorithm is known as divide and conquer.

When using recusion it is important to add a base case that tells your method when to terminate.
